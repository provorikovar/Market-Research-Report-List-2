<p><p>The Hipot test, also known as the dielectric withstand test or insulation resistance test, is a commonly used electrical safety test performed on electronic equipment and components. It evaluates the ability of the device under test (DUT) to withstand high voltage levels without breakdown. The test is conducted by applying a high voltage, typically 1.5 to 5 kilovolts, between the energized conductors and the ground, while measuring the leakage current.</p><p>The Hipot test is an essential quality control test in various industries, including electronics, electrical, and automotive, to ensure the safety and reliability of products. It helps identify defects in insulation systems, such as hairline cracks, faulty insulation, or inadequate spacing, which could lead to hazardous situations, such as electric shocks or fire accidents.</p><p>The global Hipot test market is experiencing significant growth due to the increasing demand for consumer electronics, rising safety concerns, and stringent regulatory standards.
