<p><p>Thumb braces can be classified into two main types: splints and support straps. Splints are rigid braces that provide stability and immobilization by limiting the thumb's movement. They are commonly used for treating thumb injuries or reducing pain caused by conditions like arthritis. On the other hand, support straps are adjustable and provide compression and support to the thumb. They are often used to relieve pain and provide comfort during activities that involve constant thumb movement, such as sports or repetitive motions.</p></p>
