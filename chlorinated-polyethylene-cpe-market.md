<p><p>Chlorinated Polyethylene (CPE) is a thermoplastic elastomer made by chlorination process of high-density polyethylene (HDPE) through a low-temperature process. It possesses excellent physical and chemical properties, including high flexibility, resistance to chemicals, UV radiation, and heat. CPE is widely used in various industries such as automotive, construction, wire and cable, and consumer goods.</p><p>The global chlorinated polyethylene market is expected to witness substantial growth during the forecast period. The market growth is mainly driven by the increasing demand for CPE in the automotive industry for manufacturing molded parts, cables, hoses, and seals. The trend of lightweight vehicles and the need for fuel efficiency have further boosted the adoption of CPE in the automotive sector.</p><p>Additionally, the growing construction industry, particularly in emerging economies, is another key factor driving the demand for CPE. The material's resistance to extreme weather conditions and chemical properties make it suitable for applications in roofing membranes, cables, and pipes.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing focus on renewable energy sources and the development of wind energy installations have fueled the demand for CPE in the production of wind turbine blades due to its exceptional weather and abrasion resistance properties.</p><p>The Asia Pacific region dominates the global CPE market, primarily due to the presence of major automotive manufacturing hubs in countries like China, India, Japan, and South Korea.
