<p><p>An intravenous cannula, also known as an IV cannula, is a medical device used to provide access to the bloodstream for the administration of fluids, medications, or blood products. It is a thin, flexible tube typically made of plastic that is inserted into a vein, allowing healthcare professionals to infuse fluids or draw blood without having to repeatedly puncture the skin.</p><p>The intravenous cannula market is experiencing significant growth due to various factors such as the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, rising geriatric population, growing number of surgical procedures, and advancements in healthcare infrastructure. One of the prominent trends is the adoption of safety IV cannulas. These cannulas are designed to minimize the risk of needlestick injuries and infections among healthcare workers. The growing awareness regarding needlestick injuries and the implementation of safety regulations by healthcare organizations are fueling the demand for safety IV cannulas.</p><p>Another trend observed in the market is the development of technologically advanced intravenous cannulas.
