<p><p>FTTx refers to Fiber-To-The-x, a term that encompasses various fiber optic cable configurations used for delivering high-speed internet services. The two main types of FTTx cables are Single-Mode and Multi-Mode. Single-Mode cables use a single wavelength of light and are designed for long-distance transmissions, making them ideal for large-scale deployments. On the other hand, Multi-Mode cables use multiple wavelengths of light simultaneously, enabling shorter distance transmissions over larger areas. The choice of cable type depends on factors like network size, distance, and budget, with each offering unique advantages for different FTTx applications.</p></p>
