<p><ul><li>Oral Route Drugs</li><li>Parenteral Route Drugs</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Vitamin D therapy is available in two market types: oral route drugs and parenteral route drugs. Oral route drugs refer to vitamin D supplements that can be taken by mouth, such as tablets or capsules. These supplements are easily accessible and widely used by individuals wanting to improve their vitamin D levels. On the other hand, parenteral route drugs are administered through injection or infusion directly into the body. This method is often used for patients who have difficulty absorbing oral supplements or require immediate vitamin D replenishment.</p></p>
