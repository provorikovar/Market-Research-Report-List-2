There are two main types of trigger switches on the market: rocker switches and snap action switches. Rocker switches feature a hinged lever that can be rocked back and forth to turn the switch on or off. On the other hand, snap action switches are characterized by their quick and precise movements, providing a tactile feedback when triggered. Both types of switches are commonly used in various industries for controlling electrical equipment and devices.</p></p>
