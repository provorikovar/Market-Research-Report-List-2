<p><p>The biogas plant market consists of two main types: wet digestion and dry digestion. Wet digestion involves the anaerobic decomposition of organic waste in the presence of water. This process typically involves a higher moisture content and is suitable for materials like sewage sludge, animal manure, and food waste. Dry digestion, on the other hand, involves the decomposition of organic waste without the addition of water. This process is suitable for materials like crop residues and agricultural waste, which have a lower moisture content. Both wet and dry digestion processes contribute to the production of biogas, a renewable energy source.</p></p>
