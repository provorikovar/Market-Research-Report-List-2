<p><strong>The Industrial Cartridge Valves Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Screw-in Cartridge Valve</li><li>Slip-in Cartridge Valve</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Industrial cartridge valves are hydraulic components commonly used in industrial machinery and equipment. There are two main types of industrial cartridge valves: screw-in cartridge valve and slip-in cartridge valve.</p><p>Screw-in cartridge valves are designed to be threaded directly into a hydraulic manifold or block. They require a threaded port connection and are known for their easy installation and compact design. They are commonly used in applications where space is limited.</p><p>Slip-in cartridge valves, on the other hand, are inserted into a cavity within a hydraulic manifold or block. They do not require threaded ports and are secured in place by a retaining mechanism. Slip-in cartridge valves offer flexibility in design and are often used when multiple valves need to be integrated into a single manifold.</p><p>Both types of cartridge valves are essential in controlling the flow and pressure of hydraulic fluid within industrial machinery, allowing for precise and efficient operation.</p></p>
