<p><p>Leadless pacing systems are advanced medical technologies designed to regulate the electrical signaling of the heart without the need for traditional pacing leads. These systems are categorized into various types based on the specific heart condition they treat. Atrial Fibrillation leadsless pacing systems are used to correct irregular heartbeats in the atria. Sinus Node Dysfunction systems are utilized when the sinus node fails to generate electrical impulses properly. Atrioventricular Block pacing systems address disrupted electrical signals between the atria and ventricles. Lastly, "Others" refer to leadless pacing systems developed for rare or less common heart conditions.</p></p>
