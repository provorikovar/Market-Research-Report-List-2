<p><p>A mini C-arm is a medical imaging device that consists of a fluoroscope and an X-ray generator mounted on a C-shaped arm. It is smaller in size compared to a conventional C-arm, making it more portable and ideal for usage in clinics, hospitals, and ambulatory surgical centers. Mini C-arms provide high-resolution X-ray images in real-time, aiding in the diagnosis and treatment of various orthopedic conditions such as fractures, dislocations, and bone infections.</p><p>The mini C-arm market is witnessing significant growth due to several factors. The increasing prevalence of orthopedic disorders and the growing geriatric population are driving the demand for mini C-arms. Additionally, technological advancements such as the development of mini C-arm systems with higher image quality and improved visualization are further fueling market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the rising preference for minimally invasive procedures is also a key factor contributing to the market growth. Mini C-arms enable surgeons to perform orthopedic surgeries with smaller incisions, resulting in reduced pain, shorter recovery time, and improved patient outcomes.</p><p>The market for mini C-arms is also being driven by the increasing adoption of these devices in emerging economies. <p><p>The Mini C-Arm market consists of two main types - Mini C-Arm with Pulsed Fluoroscopy and Mini C-Arm with Continuous Fluoroscopy. Mini C-Arm with Pulsed Fluoroscopy is a type of imaging technology that uses intermittent X-ray beams, resulting in lower radiation exposure for patients and medical professionals. On the other hand, Mini C-Arm with Continuous Fluoroscopy uses a continuous stream of X-ray beams, allowing for real-time imaging during procedures. Both types offer effective imaging solutions for orthopedic and surgical procedures, with different radiation approaches.</p></p>
