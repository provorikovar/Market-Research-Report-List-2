<p><p>Fully fluorinated fluoropolymers are a type of synthetic polymer known for their exceptional chemical resistance and heat resistance. They are commonly used in various industries for their non-stick properties and electrical insulation capabilities. The market for fully fluorinated fluoropolymers can be divided into three main types:</p><p>1. PTFE (Polytetrafluoroethylene): PTFE is a white solid with a low coefficient of friction, making it an ideal material for non-stick coatings in cookware and as a lubricant in various applications. It also has excellent resistance to chemicals and high temperatures.</p><p>2. FEP (Fluorinated Ethylene Propylene): FEP is a transparent and flexible fluoropolymer with similar properties to PTFE. It is commonly used in the production of wire insulation, tubing, and films due to its excellent electrical insulation properties and resistance to heat and chemicals.</p><p>3. PFA (Perfluoroalkoxy): PFA is a fully fluorinated polymer that combines the properties of PTFE and FEP. It is widely used in the semiconductor, pharmaceutical, and chemical industries for its excellent resistance to high temperatures, chemicals, and UV radiation. PFA is often used in the production of lining materials, gaskets, and electrical cables.</p></p>
