<p><p>ROADM WSS (Reconfigurable Optical Add-Drop Multiplexer Wavelength Selective Switch) components are used in optical networking to enable flexible and dynamic routing of wavelengths. There are three main types of ROADM WSS components: Blocker-Based, PLC-Based, and Wavelength Selective Switches. </p><p>Blocker-Based devices use high-speed optical switches to block or pass certain wavelengths. </p><p>PLC-Based devices utilize planar lightwave circuits to select and reroute wavelengths. </p><p>Wavelength Selective Switches act as a central hub, allowing wavelengths to be added, dropped, or switched between input and output fibers. </p><p>These components are crucial in edge markets, where data traffic is processed and transmitted closer to the end-users, ensuring faster and more efficient network connectivity.</p></p>
