<p><p>Gaucher disease is a rare genetic disorder that affects the functioning of the enzyme glucocerebrosidase, resulting in the accumulation of a substance called glucocerebroside in certain organs, particularly the liver, spleen, and bone marrow. This leads to a wide range of symptoms including enlarged organs, bone pain, anemia, and fatigue.</p><p>Currently, the treatment options for Gaucher disease primarily involve enzyme replacement therapy (ERT) and substrate reduction therapy (SRT). ERT involves replacing the missing or deficient enzyme with a synthetic version, while SRT aims to reduce the production of glucocerebroside in the body.</p><p>The Gaucher disease drugs market has witnessed steady growth in recent years and is expected to continue this trend during the forecast period.
