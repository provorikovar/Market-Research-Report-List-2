<p><p>Cardiac marker testing is a medical diagnostic tool that helps in the identification and measurement of various substances present in the blood, indicating heart-related conditions. There are different types of cardiac marker testing methods available, including chemiluminescence, immunofluorescence, ELISA (enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay), immunochromatography, and others. Chemiluminescence testing employs the emission of light from a chemical reaction, while immunofluorescence utilizes fluorescent antibodies to detect specific markers. ELISA measures the presence of antibodies or antigens using enzymes. Immunochromatography relies on the movement of labeled antibodies through a strip. Other methods may include molecular testing techniques like PCR (polymerase chain reaction) or genetic assays.</p></p>
