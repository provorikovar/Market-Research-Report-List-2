<p><p>Satellite telephones are devices providing communication services through satellites orbiting around the Earth. The market is divided into two types: LEO (Low Earth Orbit) and GEO (Geostationary Orbit) satellite telephones. LEO satellite telephones operate using satellites in close proximity to the Earth's surface, resulting in lower latency but requiring a larger number of satellites for global coverage. GEO satellite telephones use satellites positioned at high altitudes, which offer global coverage but higher latency. Both market types cater to the need for remote communication where terrestrial networks are unavailable or unreliable.</p></p>
