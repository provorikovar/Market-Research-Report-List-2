<p><p>The dried herbs market consists of two main types: whole herbs and powdered herbs. Whole herbs refer to herbs that are dried and packaged in their complete and intact form, maintaining their natural appearance. This type is often preferred by consumers who enjoy the process of grinding or crushing the herbs themselves before use, as it allows for maximum flavor and aroma. Powdered herbs, on the other hand, are herbs that have been ground into a fine powder before packaging. This type is popular among consumers who prioritize convenience and prefer easily measurable and mixable herbs for cooking and other applications.</p></p>
