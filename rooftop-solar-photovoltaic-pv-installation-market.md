<p><ul><li>Crystalline Silicon PV</li><li>Thin Film PV</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The rooftop solar photovoltaic (PV) installation market is divided into two types: crystalline silicon PV and thin film PV. Crystalline silicon PV is the most common and widely used type, employing silicon wafers to convert sunlight into electricity. It offers high efficiency but is more expensive. On the other hand, thin film PV uses a thinner semiconductor layer, resulting in a lower cost and easier installation. However, it is less efficient and requires more space. Both types contribute to the growth of rooftop solar PV installations.</p></p>
