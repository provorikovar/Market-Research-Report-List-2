<p><p>Ascites is a condition that causes the accumulation of fluid in the abdomen, leading to swelling and discomfort. It is commonly associated with liver cirrhosis, but can also occur due to other causes such as cancer, heart failure, kidney disease, and infections. The main goal of ascites treatment is to relieve symptoms, manage the underlying cause, and prevent complications.</p><p>The treatment options for ascites include lifestyle changes, medication, therapeutic procedures, and in severe cases, surgical intervention. Lifestyle changes may involve reducing salt intake, restricting fluid intake, and avoiding alcohol. Medications such as diuretics are commonly prescribed to eliminate excess fluid and reduce swelling. Paracentesis is a therapeutic procedure in which the fluid is drained from the abdomen using a needle, providing immediate relief. Other procedures like shunt placement or transjugular intrahepatic portosystemic shunt (TIPS) may be recommended in certain cases. In severe cases, a liver transplant may be necessary.</p><p>The Ascites Treatment Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.2% during the forecast period.
