<p><p>Oscilloscopes are electronic instruments used for observing and analyzing electrical signals. There are two main types: analog oscilloscopes and digital oscilloscopes. Analog oscilloscopes use cathode-ray tubes to display waveform patterns and allow for real-time analysis of signals. On the other hand, digital oscilloscopes convert analog signals into digital format, offering more advanced features such as data storage, mathematical analysis, and waveform manipulation. Both types serve the purpose of signal analysis, but digital oscilloscopes have gained popularity due to their enhanced functionalities and ease of use in modern technological applications.</p></p>
