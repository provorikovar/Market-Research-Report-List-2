<p><p>Veneers in dentistry are thin shells made to cover the front surface of teeth, enhancing their appearance and protecting them from damage. There are mainly two types of veneer materials: composite and dental porcelain. Composite veneers are made of a tooth-colored resin material directly applied to the tooth and shaped to match the desired look. Dental porcelain veneers, on the other hand, are thin ceramic shells custom-made and bonded to the tooth surface. Both types have their own advantages and are widely used in cosmetic dentistry to improve smiles.</p></p>
