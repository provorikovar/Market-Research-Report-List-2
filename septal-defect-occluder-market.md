<p><p>A septal defect occluder is a medical device used to close a septal defect, which is an abnormal opening in the septum, the dividing wall between the left and right sides of the heart. This condition disrupts the normal blood flow in the heart, causing various health problems. The occluder is inserted through a minimally invasive procedure and placed in the defect to close it, allowing for proper blood circulation.</p><p>The septal defect occluder market is expected to witness significant growth in the forecast period. Factors contributing to this growth include the increasing prevalence of congenital heart diseases and the rising demand for minimally invasive procedures. Additionally, technological advancements in occluder devices and increased healthcare expenditure are driving market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the septal defect occluder market is the development of bioresorbable occluders. These occluders are designed to gradually dissolve in the body over time, eliminating the need for surgical removal. This innovative technology provides better patient outcomes and reduces the risk of complications associated with permanent occluders.</p><p>Another emerging trend is the use of transcatheter devices for septal defect closure. Transcatheter techniques offer a less invasive alternative to open-heart surgeries, reducing recovery time and complications for patients.
