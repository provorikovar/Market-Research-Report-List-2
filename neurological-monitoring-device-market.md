<p><p>Neurological monitoring devices are classified into two types: non-invasive and invasive. Non-invasive devices collect data from the body's surface and do not require any penetration or surgery, making them less risky and more comfortable for patients. They are commonly used for routine monitoring in clinics and hospitals. On the other hand, invasive devices involve the insertion of probes or sensors into the body, enabling direct measurement of physiological parameters. These devices are typically used in critical care settings or for more complex diagnostic procedures.</p></p>
