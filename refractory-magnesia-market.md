<p><p>Refractory magnesia is a type of mineral that is widely used in the production of refractory materials for high-temperature applications. There are three main types of refractory magnesia: fused magnesia, dead burned magnesia, and others. Fused magnesia is produced by melting raw magnesite at high temperatures, resulting in a dense and high-purity material. Dead burned magnesia is obtained by calcining magnesite in rotary kilns, resulting in a more chemically stable product. Lastly, the "others" category includes variations like caustic calcined magnesia, electrofused magnesia, and synthetic magnesia. Each type has specific properties that make them suitable for different refractory applications.</p></p>
