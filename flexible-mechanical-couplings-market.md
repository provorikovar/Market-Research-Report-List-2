<p><p>Flexible mechanical couplings are widely used in various industries for connecting shafts and transmitting torque. They come in three main types: sleeve, flanged, and clamped. </p><p>- Sleeve couplings are simple and cost-effective, consisting of a hollow cylindrical sleeve that connects two shafts. These couplings allow for misalignment compensation and can transmit moderate torques.</p><p>- Flanged couplings have flanges on both sides, providing high torque transmission and good misalignment compensation. They are robust and commonly used in heavy-duty applications.</p><p>- Clamped couplings use clamping mechanisms to secure the coupling to the shafts, offering excellent torque transmission, misalignment compensation, and ease of installation. These couplings are favored for precision applications requiring high torque transmission.</p></p>
