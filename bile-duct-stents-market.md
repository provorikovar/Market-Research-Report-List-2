<p><p>Bile duct stents are medical devices used to treat blockages in the bile ducts, which can occur due to various conditions such as gallstones, tumors, or strictures. These stents are basically tiny tubes made of metal, plastic, or a combination of both, and they are inserted into the bile ducts to keep them open and allow the free flow of bile from the liver to the intestine.</p><p>The bile duct stents market has been experiencing steady growth in recent years. The increasing prevalence of conditions that cause bile duct blockages, such as gallstone disease and pancreatic cancer, is one of the primary factors driving the market growth. Additionally, the rising adoption of minimally invasive procedures for the treatment of biliary disorders is also contributing to the market expansion.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements and product innovations in bile duct stents are boosting market growth. For instance, the development of fully covered self-expandable metal stents (FCSEMS) has improved stent placement and reduced the risk of post-procedural complications.</p><p>The emergence of biodegradable stents is another trend in the bile duct stents market. <p><ul><li>Covered Type</li><li>Uncovered Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Bile duct stents are used to treat blockages in the bile ducts, which can occur due to various conditions. Covered type stents have an outer coating that helps prevent tissue growth and decrease the risk of stent blockage. These stents are typically made of materials like silicone or polyurethane. On the other hand, uncovered type stents do not have an outer coating and are made of materials like metal or plastic. Uncovered stents are often used when tissue growth is not a concern or when there is a need for long-term stent placement.</p></p>
