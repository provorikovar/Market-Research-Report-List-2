<p><p>Immersion temperature sensors are widely used in various industries to measure and monitor the temperature of liquids and gases. Two common types of immersion temperature sensors are resistance sensors and thermocouple sensors. Resistance sensors function by measuring changes in electrical resistance as temperature changes, while thermocouple sensors generate a voltage proportional to the temperature difference between two junctions. Both types of sensors are reliable and accurate in measuring temperature, but have slight differences in their working principles and applications.</p></p>
