<p><p>A scintillation camera, also known as a gamma camera, is a medical imaging device used to detect gamma radiation emitted by radioactive substances within the body. It consists of a scintillation crystal, a photomultiplier tube, and electronic components. The crystal generates flashes of light when it interacts with gamma rays, which are then detected and converted into electrical signals by the photomultiplier tube. These signals are processed to create an image that shows the distribution of the radioactive substance in the body.</p><p>The scintillation camera market is expected to exhibit significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of cancer and cardiovascular diseases, as well as the rising adoption of nuclear medicine for diagnostic purposes, are driving the market growth. Scintillation cameras are widely used in nuclear medicine for the diagnosis and staging of various diseases, including cancer, as they provide functional and anatomical information about the organs and tissues.</p><p>In addition, technological advancements in scintillation cameras, such as the development of hybrid imaging systems that combine single-photon emission computed tomography (SPECT) and positron emission tomography (PET), are further fueling the market growth. These hybrid systems offer better imaging quality, higher sensitivity, and improved quantification accuracy, leading to increased adoption in clinical settings.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing geriatric population, which is more susceptible to chronic diseases, and the growing demand for non-invasive diagnostic procedures are expected to drive the market growth in the coming years.</p><p>Overall, the scintillation camera market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 6.9% during the forecast period.</p></p>
