<p><p>Integrated Circuit Optical Couplers, also known as optocouplers or optoisolators, are electronic devices that transmit signals between two isolated circuits using light absorption and emission. There are two main types of these couplers: Nonlinear Photoelectric Couplers and Linear Photoelectric Couplers. Nonlinear Photoelectric Couplers operate by converting the input optical signal to a current or voltage output with non-linear characteristics, while Linear Photoelectric Couplers produce a linear response, accurately reproducing the input signal. These couplers find applications in telecommunications, industrial control systems, medical equipment, and more, ensuring signal isolation and preventing electrical interference.</p></p>
