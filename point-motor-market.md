<p><p>Point motors are devices used in railway signaling systems to control the movement of points or switches. There are two main types: electro-hydraulic and electric point motors. Electro-hydraulic motors use a combination of electric and hydraulic power to operate the points. They are versatile and suitable for both low and high-speed railway lines. On the other hand, electric point motors rely solely on electrical power and are commonly used in urban transit systems. They offer fast response times and are more compact in size compared to electro-hydraulic ones.</p></p>
