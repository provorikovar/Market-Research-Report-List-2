<p><p>Biofuel cells are a type of fuel cell that use biological organisms or enzymes to convert chemical energy into electrical energy. Microbial fuel cells (MFCs) use microorganisms, such as bacteria, to generate electricity through the breakdown of organic waste. Enzymatic biofuel cells (EBFCs) employ enzymes as catalysts to facilitate the conversion of fuel into electricity. Both MFCs and EBFCs offer sustainable and environmentally friendly alternatives to traditional fuel cells, as they utilize natural resources and produce minimal pollutants. These market types are gaining attention for their potential in various applications, including wastewater treatment and portable electronics.</p></p>
