<p><p>Complex Programmable Logic Devices (CPLDs) are semiconductor devices used to design digital electronic circuits. They can be classified into various market types based on their memory technology. EEPROM based CPLDs utilize Electrically Erasable Programmable Read-Only Memory to store configurations, providing flexibility and reprogrammability. Flash-based CPLDs offer non-volatile memory using Flash technology, allowing for easy updates and reconfiguration. Other market types include CPLDs that employ Antifuse technology or Fuse-Link technology for configuration storage. These different market types offer diverse options for designers to choose from based on their specific requirements and preferences.</p></p>
