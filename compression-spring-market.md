<p><p>Compression springs are a type of mechanical spring used in various industries. They come in different shapes to suit specific applications. Conical compression springs have a tapering design, offering variable resistance along their length. Hourglass springs have a narrow center section, enabling them to compress with less force compared to their ends. Barrel-shaped springs have a larger diameter at the center, allowing increased deflection. These different types of compression springs ensure optimal performance and provide varied load-bearing capabilities based on the requirements of a particular market or application.</p></p>
