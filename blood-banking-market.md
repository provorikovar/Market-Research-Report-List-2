<p><p>Blood banking refers to the process of collecting, testing, and storing blood for future use. It involves storing blood components such as red blood cells, platelets, plasma, and cryoprecipitate, which can then be transfused to patients in need. Blood banking is crucial for various medical procedures like surgeries, trauma care, organ transplants, and treatment of various diseases.</p><p>The global blood banking market has been exhibiting significant growth due to increasing demand for blood transfusions, rising prevalence of hematological disorders, and advancements in technology. The market is also driven by the growing number of accidents and trauma cases that require blood transfusions. Moreover, the rising prevalence of chronic diseases such as cancer, cardiovascular diseases, and autoimmune disorders has further fueled the demand for blood components.</p><p>The market has witnessed several technological advancements in recent years, including the automation of blood collection and processing procedures, improved storage techniques, and the introduction of blood tracking systems. These advancements have enhanced the efficiency, safety, and accuracy of blood banking processes, thereby driving the market growth.</p><p>In addition, the increasing trend of cord blood banking has also contributed to market growth. Cord blood is collected from the umbilical cord after childbirth and contains valuable hematopoietic stem cells that can be used for transplantation in the treatment of various diseases. The demand for cord blood banking has been rising due to the growing awareness about the potential benefits of stem cell therapies.</p><p>Overall, the blood banking market is expected to continue its growth trajectory in the coming years, with a projected CAGR of 9% during the forecast period. Factors such as increasing awareness about the importance of blood donation, technological advancements, and the rising prevalence of diseases necessitating blood transfusions are likely to drive market growth.</p></p>
