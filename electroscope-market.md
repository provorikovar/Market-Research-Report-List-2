<p><p>Electroscopes are devices used to detect the presence and magnitude of electric charge. There are different market types of electroscopes: Pith-Ball and Gold-Leaf. Pith-Ball electroscopes consist of a small ball of pith suspended on a string. When charged, the ball is attracted or repelled, indicating the presence and nature of the charge. Gold-Leaf electroscopes use a thin strip of gold leaf attached to a metal rod. When charged, the gold leaf deflects, allowing for charge detection. Both types serve as effective and widely-used tools in the field of static electricity experimentation and demonstrations.</p></p>
