<p><p>A four-way reversing valve is an essential component of a heat pump system, allowing it to switch between heating and cooling modes. There are two main types of four-way reversing valves: slide-type and poppet-type. </p><p>In slide-type valves, a sliding mechanism is used to redirect the flow of refrigerant, enabling the heat pump to switch between heating and cooling modes. On the other hand, poppet-type valves use a system of small poppet valves to control the direction of refrigerant flow. Both types serve the same purpose of reversing the flow of refrigerant, but they have different mechanisms for achieving this.</p></p>
