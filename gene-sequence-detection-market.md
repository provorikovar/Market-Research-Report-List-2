<p><ul><li>Emulsion PCR</li><li>Bridge Amplification</li><li>Single-Molecule</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Gene Sequence Detection Market Types: </p><p>1. Emulsion PCR: This method involves isolating single DNA molecules into microscopic water-in-oil droplets, where each droplet serves as an individual reaction chamber. In these compartments, millions of DNA molecules can be amplified simultaneously, increasing the efficiency of gene sequencing.</p><p>2. Bridge Amplification: This type of market technique involves bridging DNA fragments onto a solid surface like a glass slide or a flow cell. Through this process, DNA templates are attached to the surface and amplified, enabling the detection of gene sequences.</p><p>3. Single-Molecule Market: This market focuses on detecting gene sequences at the level of individual molecules, allowing for more accurate identification and analysis. By directly observing and analyzing single DNA molecules, this method offers greater resolution and sensitivity in gene sequence detection.</p></p>
