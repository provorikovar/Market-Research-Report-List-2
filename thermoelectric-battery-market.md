<p><p>A thermoelectric battery, also known as a thermoelectric generator (TEG), is a device that converts temperature differences into electricity. It utilizes the phenomena of thermoelectric effect, which occurs when a temperature gradient is applied across two different materials that are connected electrically. The thermoelectric battery consists of multiple pairs of p-type and n-type semiconductor materials, which generate electricity when heated by an external heat source.</p><p>The thermoelectric battery market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for clean and sustainable energy sources, coupled with the rising need for waste heat recovery across various industries, is driving the market growth. Additionally, the growing awareness about the depletion of fossil fuels and the need for energy conservation is further fueling the adoption of thermoelectric batteries.</p><p>Another major factor contributing to the market growth is the rapid advancements in thermoelectric material technology. Researchers and manufacturers are constantly focusing on the development of advanced materials with higher conversion efficiencies, improved durability, and cost-effectiveness. These advancements are expected to enhance the overall performance of thermoelectric batteries, further driving their market demand.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing adoption of thermoelectric batteries in various applications such as automotive, aerospace, industrial, and consumer electronics sectors is expected to boost the market growth. The ability of thermoelectric batteries to provide uninterrupted power supply in remote and off-grid locations is also driving their adoption across different industries.</p><p>In conclusion, the thermoelectric battery market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 12.5% during the forecast period.
