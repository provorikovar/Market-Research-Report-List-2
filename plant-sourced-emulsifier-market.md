<p><p>The plant-sourced emulsifier market consists of various types, including lecithin, sorbitan esters, stearoyl lactylates, and others. Lecithin is a natural emulsifier derived from sources like soybeans, and it helps stabilize oil and water-based products. Sorbitan esters are derived from sorbitol, a sugar alcohol, and they are commonly used in food and cosmetic applications. Stearoyl lactylates are derived from lactic acid and fatty acids, and they enhance the texture and stability of food products. Other plant-based emulsifiers include gums, mucilages, and proteins, which are used in various industries for emulsification purposes.</p></p>
