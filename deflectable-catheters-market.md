<p><p>Deflectable catheters are medical devices used in minimally invasive procedures to navigate through intricate anatomical structures. Three types are prominent in the market. Unidirectional deflectable catheters can be bent in one direction, allowing for precise navigation through specific areas. Bidirectional deflectable catheters can flex in two directions, providing enhanced maneuverability in complex anatomy. Multidirectional deflectable catheters offer the highest level of flexibility, capable of bending in multiple directions, enabling access to challenging anatomical regions. These catheters provide physicians with greater control and precision during procedures, improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
