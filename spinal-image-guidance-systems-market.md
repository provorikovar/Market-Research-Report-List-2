<p><p>Spinal Image Guidance Systems refer to the technology used during spinal surgeries to provide real-time imaging and guidance to surgeons. There are several types of systems available in the market, including X-ray, Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), Computed Tomography (CT), and 3D Fluoroscopy Technique. X-ray systems use electromagnetic waves to produce detailed images of the spinal structure. MRI systems use a magnetic field and radio waves to generate high-resolution images. CT systems employ a series of X-ray images taken from different angles to create cross-sectional images of the spine. 3D Fluoroscopy Technique combines real-time 3D imaging with fluoroscopy to provide precise guidance during spinal procedures.</p></p>
