<p><p>Synthetic quartz glass for optics is used in various industries due to its exceptional optical properties. The two popular market types in the industry are CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition) process and Plasma Deposition. In the CVD process, a quartz precursor is deposited onto a substrate through a chemical reaction. This method allows for high-quality, uniform layers of synthetic quartz glass to be produced. On the other hand, Plasma Deposition utilizes plasma to deposit quartz on a substrate, creating thin uniform layers with excellent adhesion. Both processes are essential in the manufacture of high-quality synthetic quartz glass for optics.</p></p>
